Privacy & Cookie Policy

How Hale & Associates collects, uses, and protects your information.

General

Hale & Associates ("H&A," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of consumers and customers—including visitors to our United States websites and digital properties. This Privacy & Cookie Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and safeguard personal information and how we employ cookies and similar technologies on H&A‑owned websites and subsites.

Any future changes to this policy will be posted at https://www.haleandassociates.com/privacy. Links to this policy appear in the footer of every page.

By using any H&A website you accept the practices described in this Privacy & Cookie Policy. Your continued use after we post changes constitutes acceptance of those changes.

Glossary

"Consumer" – an individual (or legal representative) who obtains or seeks to obtain a financial product or service for personal, household, or family purposes.

"Customer" – a consumer with whom H&A has an ongoing advisory or financial service relationship.

"Nonpublic personal information" – personally identifiable financial information that is not publicly available, including any list or grouping of such information.

Information H&A Collects

  • H&A collects contact details (name, postal address, telephone number, email) and investment‑related information from individuals who request our informational materials.
  • Phone calls and electronic communications may be recorded or monitored for quality‑assurance and training.
  • Résumé and application data submitted through our careers portal are collected for recruiting.
  • Information provided orally or in writing (email, mail, online forms, in person) is collected from prospective clients, clients, applicants, and employees.
  • Our sites use cookies and similar technologies to gather technical data such as IP address, browser type, operating system, referring site, pages visited, and inferences drawn from this data.

How H&A Uses and Shares Information

  • Internal record‑keeping, analytics, and regulatory compliance.
  • Improving our products, services, and digital experience.
  • Quality‑assurance, security monitoring, and staff training.
  • Following up via email, phone, or mail with people who request materials.
  • Sending periodic communications about services, developments, or events we believe may interest you.
  • Customising and optimising marketing, including digital advertising.
  • Providing secure access to portions of our website.
  • Evaluating job applicants and managing employees.
  • Analyzing prospective clients' financial situations and providing advisory services.
  • Other uses permitted by law.

H&A discloses information externally as permitted or required by law—to affiliates, service providers (including cloud‑hosting and analytics vendors), regulatory authorities, or when you direct us to do so.

Cookie Policy

A cookie is a small text file downloaded to your device when you access a webpage that uses cookies. We categorise cookies as follows:

  • Functionality cookies – remember choices you make, such as region or text size.
  • Personalisation cookies – remember information that changes page appearance or behaviour.
  • Analytics cookies – collect pseudonymised information on how you interact with our sites.
  • Advertising cookies – allow us and our partners to deliver ads relevant to your interests.

Third‑Party Scheduling Tools

Our website uses Calendly, a third‑party scheduling service provided by Calendly LLC, to allow you to book consultations directly from our site. When you interact with the Calendly scheduling widget on our website, Calendly may set cookies and collect personal information (such as your name, email address, and scheduling preferences) in accordance with its own privacy policy.

We have opted to hide Calendly's separate cookie‑consent banner because our website already addresses cookie consent. By using the scheduling feature on our website, you acknowledge that Calendly may place cookies on your device for functionality, analytics, and personalisation purposes. You can manage or delete these cookies through your browser settings as described in the "How to Manage Cookies" section above.

For more information on how Calendly handles your data, please review Calendly's Privacy Policy and Calendly's Cookie Policy.
