Brand Shapes

Use subtle, sweeping curves in the background of documents — flyers, one-pagers, slides, and printouts. These shapes give pages visual presence and polish without competing with content. Always use brand green (#228244) at low opacity (3–7%).

Brand Swoosh
Are You Getting the Most From Your Retirement Benefits?
Personalized retirement planning for Teamsters Local 294 retirees.
A single wide swoosh sitting low on the page. Keeps the top clean for content and grounds the document visually. Use on flyers, one-pagers, presentations, and printouts.
Usage
Color: Always brand green (#228244) at 3–7% opacity on light backgrounds. On dark backgrounds, use white (#FFFFFF) at 5–10% opacity.
Scale: Shapes should be large — spanning at least a third of the page. Small shapes look decorative rather than structural.
Placement: Position shapes so they don't overlap with key content areas. The swoosh should feel like part of the page, not layered on top of text.
Don'ts
  • ✕Don't use sharp geometric shapes (triangles, hexagons) — they conflict with the flowing brand
  • ✕Don't use high-opacity fills — shapes should never compete with content
  • ✕Don't use colors other than brand green or white for background shapes
  • ✕Don't scale shapes too small — they lose their purpose and become visual noise
