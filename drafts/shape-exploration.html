Shape Exploration

Background Shapes

Large, decorative shapes that fill the background of documents — flyers, one-pagers, slides, and printouts. These give a blank page visual presence before any content is added. Each option below is shown as a full mock document so you can see how content sits on top.

All shapes use brand greens at low opacity so they don't compete with the content. The goal: when someone picks up a Hale document, it looks designed and intentional, not like a Word template.

What follows:
1 — Sweeping ribbon curves (inspired by the logo's flowing form)
2 — Broad gradient wave, top-right corner
3 — Layered arcs — concentric curves radiating from a corner
4 — Single bold swoosh across the page
5 — Soft gradient blobs — organic, modern
6 — Minimal — thin flowing lines only
Option 1
Sweeping Ribbon Curves
Multiple flowing curves sweeping from top-right to bottom-left, like ribbons. Echoes the logo's wave form. Most expressive option.
